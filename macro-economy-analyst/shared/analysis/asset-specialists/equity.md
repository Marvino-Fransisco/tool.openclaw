# Equity Market Analysis
**As-of:** 2026-04-06
**Analyst:** Equity Strategist (Tier-2 Agent)

## Executive Summary

- **Stagflationary regime is the base case.** The Iran-US conflict has pushed WTI to $111.62 (+84% YoY), creating a supply-side inflation shock that constrains the Fed while dragging growth. Equities face a toxic macro backdrop: rising rates, widening credit spreads, elevated oil, and extreme fear.
- **Equity risk-reward is poor near-term.** S&P 500 at 6,583 (-4.7% MoM, -6.2% from 52W high) with RSI at 47 — not yet oversold despite Fear & Greed at 13. The market is pricing a bad outcome but not the worst. Tuesday's Hormuz deadline (Apr 7-8) is the binary event that determines near-term direction.
- **Earnings season (mid-April) is the next fundamental test.** Guidance revisions for oil cost pass-through, margin compression, and capex cuts will be critical. Downward revisions are likely.
- **Defense and energy are the only clear equity winners** under current regime. Growth-sensitive sectors (tech, consumer discretionary, industrials) face headwinds from higher discount rates and demand destruction.
- **Contrarian buying is premature.** Extreme fear (13/100) is typically a buy signal, but this is driven by an active geopolitical shock with no clear resolution catalyst. Wait for either oil stabilization below $100 or a diplomatic breakthrough before adding risk.

## Macro-Equity Correlation Assessment

### Monetary Policy → Equities: **Negative**
The Fed is trapped. FOMC March dots project 3.4% year-end — almost certainly too low given the oil shock. The Fed cannot cut into rising inflation without credibility damage, and cannot hike without recession risk. Result: **prolonged hold at restrictive levels**, which compresses equity multiples and keeps financial conditions tight. The 2Y at 3.79% (+25bps MoM) confirms markets are repricing for fewer cuts. Real yields are compressed (nominal yields rising but inflation expectations rising faster), which is the worst of both worlds — no rate relief, no inflation protection from bonds.

### Inflation → Equities: **Negative**
Oil at $111 feeds directly into headline CPI via energy and transportation costs, with secondary effects on core inflation through input costs and rent channels. The FOMC's 2.4% PCE projection is stale — expect upward revisions. Headline CPI likely breaches 4%+ within 2 months if current oil levels hold. **Multiple compression** is the primary transmission channel: higher inflation → higher discount rates → lower P/E. Historically, equities underperform significantly when CPI is above 3.5% and accelerating.

### Growth → Equities: **Mixed-to-Negative**
GDP projections are being revised down from 2.3% (FOMC median) to 1.5-2.0% as the oil shock acts as a regressive tax on consumers. Retail sales data (7.3-7.7% 5Y CAGR) predates the shock and will likely deteriorate. Labor market at 4.3% unemployment is a point of strength but vulnerable. **Earnings growth estimates are too high** — consensus hasn't fully priced in the oil shock margin compression. Q1 earnings guidance will be the wake-up call.

### Fiscal Policy → Equities: **Mixed**
Defense spending surge benefits a narrow set of equities (defense/aerospace). However, the unfunded fiscal shock (war costs + potential energy subsidies) adds to deficit concerns, supporting term premium expansion (30Y at 4.88%). Net effect: **positive for defense, negative for broad equities** via higher long-term rates and fiscal sustainability concerns.

### Global Markets → Equities: **Negative**
DXY +2.5% MoM creates tightening financial conditions for EM and multinationals. EUR/USD and GBP/USD weakness signals European energy vulnerability — a negative read-through for US multinationals with European revenue exposure. China slowdown risk compounds the global demand picture. Bitcoin underperforming (-12% YoY) confirms risk assets are out of favor.

### Liquidity & Flows → Equities: **Negative**
QT continues (WALCL $6.68T, modestly rising). RRP facility near-drained ($0.33T, -87% YoY) — limited liquidity buffer. Credit stress is building (HY OAS 317bps, +38bps YoY) — if this breaches 400bps, forced selling from leveraged funds could create a liquidity cascade. Dollar strength is doing autonomous tightening work.

### Sentiment & Risk → Equities: **Cautiously Contrarian**
Fear & Greed at 13 (extreme fear), AAII bullish at 33.6% (4.8th percentile), VIX at 24.5 (85th percentile) — sentiment is capitulatory. **Normally this is a strong contrarian buy signal, but the shock is ongoing and intensifying.** Historical parallels (1973 oil embargo, 1990 Gulf War, 2022 Russia-Ukraine) suggest that buying during the initial shock phase leads to further drawdowns before the bottom. The time to buy is when the geopolitical situation clarifies or oil rolls over, not when the shock is still accelerating.

## Equity Outlook by Time Horizon

### Near-Term (0-4 Weeks): **Cautious / Negative**

**Base case (55%):** Elevated volatility, S&P 500 range 6,200-6,800. Tuesday's Hormuz deadline creates binary event risk — escalation sends equities toward the lower bound; de-escalation triggers a relief rally toward the upper bound. Oil remains above $100. Dollar stays firm. Credit spreads continue widening gradually.

**Key risk:** Any escalation (Hormuz disruption, Iranian retaliation, regional war expansion) could push S&P 500 below 6,000 rapidly. The market is not pricing a worst-case scenario — a 10-15% additional drawdown is possible if oil breaches $130.

**Tactical stance:** Reduce equity exposure if not already defensive. Maintain commodity and gold exposure. Cash/treasuries offer real yield (4.31% on 10Y) in a high-rate environment.

### Medium-Term (1-6 Months): **Flat to Slightly Negative**

**Base case (40%):** Stagflationary drag takes hold. GDP slows to 1.5-2.0%, inflation re-accelerates to 3-3.5% headline, Fed holds steady through 2026. Equities deliver flat to low-single-digit returns as margin compression offsets revenue stability. Multiple compression of 1-2 turns from current levels.

**Key drivers:** (1) Q1 earnings season reveals margin pressure from oil costs, (2) April/May CPI prints confirm inflation re-acceleration, (3) Fed signals no cuts through H2 2026, (4) Credit spreads widen to 350-400bps.

**Bull scenario (25%):** Rapid ceasefire, oil drops below $80, Fed resumes cutting → equity rally to 7,000-7,500. This is the contrarian reward for patience.

**Bear scenario (20%):** Hormuz closure, wider regional war, oil above $150 → S&P 500 drops to 4,500-5,500, recession begins, credit crisis unfolds.

### Long-Term (6-12+ Months): **Constructive if Conflict Resolves**

The structural equity case remains intact assuming the geopolitical shock resolves within 6 months. The US economy entered this shock with a strong labor market, moderate wage growth, and no credit excesses. Once oil stabilizes and the Fed regains policy flexibility, the path to rate cuts opens, supporting multiple expansion. However, if the conflict becomes prolonged (>12 months), the stagflationary regime could become structural, and the long-term equity thesis weakens significantly.

## Key Watch Items

1. **Strait of Hormuz — Trump's Tuesday deadline (Apr 7-8):** Iran has rejected the deadline. This is the single most important near-term event. Military escalation = equity selloff. Diplomatic progress = relief rally. No change = elevated volatility continues.

2. **Q1 Earnings Season (mid-April):** First test of how corporates are managing the oil shock. Focus on: margin guidance, energy cost pass-through, capex plans, and full-year EPS revisions. Downward revisions would confirm the stagflation thesis.

3. **April CPI Release (est. May 13):** First inflation data fully reflecting the oil shock. A hot headline print (>4%) with rising core would trap the Fed. A benign core despite hot headline would support the "transitory" narrative and improve equity outlook.

4. **HY Credit Spreads (current: 317bps):** The canary in the coal mine. A breach above 400bps would signal material financial stress and potential forced selling. Monitor daily.

5. **Oil Price Trajectory:** WTI at $111.62, RSI 69 (approaching overbought). A sustainable move above $120 would trigger deeper equity selling. A move back below $100 would significantly improve the equity outlook.

6. **Fed Communications:** Watch for Powell to address the Iran conflict's inflation implications. His silence to date is notable. Any shift in tone — hawkish or dovish — would move markets.

7. **DXY and Dollar Strength:** Current 100.14 (+2.5% MoM). Further dollar strengthening creates autonomous tightening for EM and multinationals. A DXY above 105 would be a significant equity headwind.
