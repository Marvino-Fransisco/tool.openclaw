## Executive Summary

The Fed holds a cautious, data-dependent stance as the Iran war drives an acute energy shock — WTI crude has surged +83.9% YoY to $111.62. FOMC dot plots (March 2026) signal ~3.4% year-end fed funds, with 21% hawkish vs 16% dovish speaker sentiment. Yields have steepened aggressively across the curve (+22–27 bps in the past month), credit spreads are widening (HY +38 bps YoY), and the VIX sits at 85th percentile. The dominant risk is a stagflationary scenario: war-driven inflation forcing the Fed to stay restrictive while geopolitical uncertainty suppresses growth.

## 4. Correlation Analysis

### Policy-Macro Correlations
- **Energy shock → Policy constraint:** The Iran war has created an exogenous inflationary shock that directly conflicts with the Fed's gradual easing path. The March dot plot median of 3.4% is almost certainly stale — markets are pricing higher.
- **Yield curve steepening → Growth risk:** The 3m10s steepening (+21 bps 1M) while 2s10s flattens (–5 bps) is a classic "policy dilemma" signal — front-end rates rising on inflation, long-end pressured by growth uncertainty.
- **Labor market resilience → Hawkish anchor:** At 4.3% unemployment (below the 4.4% projection), the labor market gives the Fed cover to stay restrictive.

### Policy-Market Correlations
- **DXY +2.5% MoM:** Dollar strengthening reflects both relative yield advantage and safe-haven flows. This creates a tightening financial condition independent of Fed action.
- **HY spreads widening (+20 bps MoM, +38 bps YoY):** Credit markets are pricing in recession risk from the energy shock. This is the leading indicator to watch — a HY OAS breach of 4% would signal material stress.
- **Gold off peak (–10.1% MoM) despite war:** Counterintuitive — likely reflecting margin calls in commodity positions and dollar strength. Historical parallel: gold typically lags during initial war shocks before rallying on policy response.
- **Equity drawdown –4.7% MoM:** Muted relative to oil shock magnitude. Suggests markets still price a contained conflict or rapid resolution. A WTI breach above $120 would likely trigger deeper equity selling.

### Key Relationships This Period
1. **Oil → Rates → Dollar → EM feedback loop** is active and intensifying
2. **Fed credibility gap:** March projections didn't account for war; credibility risk if inflation surprises
3. **Stagflation pricing:** Equities down, bonds down, dollar up, commodities up — textbook stagflationary asset behavior

## 5. Forward-Looking Assessment

### Market Pricing
- **Implied rate path:** 2Y at 3.79% suggests markets price ~150 bps of cuts from current levels by end-2027, but the trend is upward — not downward
- **Probability of next move:** Data insufficient for precise meeting-by-meeting pricing, but directionally — **cuts are being delayed**, not accelerated. The base case is a prolonged pause at ~4.0–4.25%

## Key Takeaways

1. **The Iran war has fundamentally altered the Fed's calculus.** March dot plots are stale — the energy shock introduces stagflation risk that makes the easing path far less certain. Expect significant dot plot revisions at the June meeting if oil remains elevated.

2. **Credit markets are the canary.** HY spreads at 317 bps (+38 bps YoY) are approaching stress territory. Watch for a breach of 400 bps as the signal that the energy shock is translating into real economic damage. IG spreads remain contained — for now.

3. **The dollar is doing the Fed's tightening work.** DXY +2.5% MoM creates autonomous financial tightening, particularly for EM economies. This is a policy-free headwind that compounds the direct effects of higher oil.

4. **Sentiment is extreme fear (Fear & Greed: 13) but fundamentals are deteriorating, not improving.** Contrarian buying signals are unreliable when the shock is ongoing and intensifying. The AAII bull-bear spread at –17.8 (4.8th percentile) is the most bearish in recent history — but the war provides no clear catalyst for reversal.

5. **Asset allocation implication:** This is a stagflationary regime until proven otherwise. Commodities (energy), gold, and selective crypto have historically outperformed in such environments. Duration risk in bonds is elevated; equity risk-reward is poor until the geopolitical situation clarifies. Cash/treasuries offer real yield in a high-rate environment but face inflation erosion.
