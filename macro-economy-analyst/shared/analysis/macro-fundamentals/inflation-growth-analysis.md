## Correlation Analysis

### Inflation ↔ Monetary Policy

The Fed is in a policy bind. The Mar 18 FOMC statement leaned hawkish, and the dot plot median keeps the fed funds rate at 3.4% through 2026 — only ~40bps below the current effective rate. Key dynamics:

- **Barr (4 speeches, leaning dovish)** and **Waller (2 speeches, 1 leaning dovish)** represent the cut camp.
- **Cook (2 speeches, 1 leaning hawkish)** anchors the inflation-concern side.
- **Powell's single speech (Mar 21)** was neutral/unclear — he has not publicly addressed the Iran conflict's inflationary implications.
- **Two hawkish-leaning FOMC statements** (Jan 28, Mar 18) suggest the committee is unwilling to ease preemptively.

**Implication:** The Fed will likely remain on hold until inflation data forces their hand — either higher (delaying cuts) or a demand shock (accelerating cuts). The oil shock tilts the balance toward delayed cuts.

### Inflation ↔ Labor Markets

- Unemployment at 4.3% aligns closely with the FOMC's 2026 projection of 4.4%.
- Average hourly earnings CAGR of 2.55% suggests moderate wage pressure — not yet a wage-price spiral concern.
- The geopolitical shock is a **supply-side** inflation event, not demand-pull. Labor markets remain a secondary transmission channel.
- **Risk:** If the conflict extends and energy costs persist, wage demands could accelerate as real purchasing power erodes.

## Risk Factors

### Upside Risks (Higher Inflation)

- **Strait of Hormuz closure/disruption** — Trump's Tuesday deadline for Iran to reopen shipping lanes; Iranian strikes on Kuwaiti infrastructure signal escalation, not de-escalation. ~20% of global oil transits Hormuz.
- **OPEC+ supply recovery lag** — OPEC+ agreed to hike output but warned of "slow recovery after attacks." Supply constraints could persist for months.
- **Second-round effects** — If energy costs embed into transportation, manufacturing, and food prices, core inflation could follow headline higher.
- **Fed credibility risk** — If the Fed cuts prematurely and inflation reaccelerates, expectations could de-anchor.

### Downside Risks (Lower Inflation / Deflationary)

- **Demand destruction** — Sustained $110+ oil could trigger a consumer spending contraction, particularly in emerging markets (Vietnam gig workers already hit).
- **China slowdown contagion** — China preparing for oil shock; a manufacturing slowdown could suppress global commodity demand.
- **Geopolitical resolution** — A sudden ceasefire or diplomatic breakthrough could reverse the oil premium rapidly.
- **Financial conditions tightening** — Rising credit spreads + elevated VIX + equity drawdowns could tighten financial conditions enough to slow the economy below potential.
