## Executive Summary

The US market regime has shifted decisively to **risk-off** driven by an active Iran-US/Israel military conflict that has disrupted global oil supply and sent energy prices surging. WTI crude at $111.62 (+83.9% YoY) and Brent at $110.35 are the primary transmission mechanism for stagflationary pressures. The S&P 500 has pulled back 6.2% from its 52-week high, the VIX sits elevated at ~24 (85th percentile), and the CNN Fear & Greed Index reads 13 (Extreme Fear). The Fed's March dot plot projects rates holding at 3.4% through 2026 with inflation only gradually returning to target — a posture that leaves little room to cut if the oil shock proves persistent. This is a **commodity-led stagflation scare** overlaying what was previously a soft-landing narrative.

## Macro Indicators

### Growth

- **FOMC March projections:** 2026 GDP median 2.3%, consistent with a stable but not accelerating expansion.
- **Retail sales:** Advance retail (RSAFS) at 738.4K and ex-auto (RSXFS) at 638.2K show ~7.3-7.7% 5Y CAGR — consumer spending remains resilient on the surface, though this data predates the oil shock.
- **Risk:** The oil price surge acts as a tax on consumers. At $110+ crude, gasoline prices will pressure discretionary spending within 2-4 weeks. Q2 GDP estimates are likely to be revised down.

### Inflation

- **CPI (CPIAUCSL):** 327.46; **Core CPI (CPILFESL):** 333.51 — both flat YoY in the data snapshot, but this reflects pre-conflict readings.
- **FOMC projections:** PCE inflation median 2.4% for 2026, 2.1% for 2027 — implying the Fed expects the current inflation run to be transitory.
- **Reality check:** With WTI up 68% in one month and Iran striking Kuwaiti oil infrastructure, a supply-driven energy price shock is already baked in. Headline CPI will likely re-accelerate in April-May prints. Core inflation may follow with a lag via input costs and rent channels.
- **Wage inflation:** AHETPI at 32.07 (+2.55% 5Y CAGR) — wage growth remains moderate, which limits the second-round inflation feedback loop.

### Labor Market

- **Unemployment rate:** 4.3%, near the Fed's 4.2% longer-run estimate. FOMC projects 4.4% for 2026.
- **Nonfarm payrolls:** 158.6M — growth trend intact but the conflict adds downside risk if energy costs force corporate cost-cutting.
- **Assessment:** Labor market remains a point of strength but is vulnerable to a demand shock from higher energy prices.

### Federal Reserve Policy

- **Current stance:** Neutral-to-slightly hawkish. The March 18 FOMC statement leaned hawkish; the March 21 Powell speech was balanced.
- **Rate path:** Fed funds median projection 3.4% for 2026, 3.1% for 2027, 3.0% longer run. Only ~50bps of cuts priced through year-end.
- **Communications breakdown (Jan 6 – Apr 6):** 63% neutral/balanced, 21% hawkish, 16% dovish. Barr (4 speeches) leans dovish; Cook (2 speeches) leans hawkish. No clear consensus direction.
- **Bind:** The Fed is in a difficult position. Cutting rates in response to an oil shock would signal tolerance of inflation; holding or hiking risks tipping the economy into recession. Expect them to hold steady and emphasize data-dependence.

## Outlook

### Near-Term (1-4 weeks)

**Base case:** Elevated volatility persists as the Iran conflict continues. Oil remains above $100, equities trade in a range (S&P 500 6,300-6,800), and the dollar stays firm. The Tuesday Hormuz deadline creates a binary event risk. Expect equity weakness early in the week if rhetoric escalates, with relief rallies on any de-escalation signals.

**Probability:** 55%

**Key driver:** Geopolitical developments in the Middle East. Economic data is secondary.

### Medium-Term (1-6 months)

**Base case:** The oil shock creates a stagflationary drag. GDP growth slows to 1.5-2.0% in H2 2026, inflation re-accelerates to 3-3.5% headline before moderating, and the Fed holds rates steady through 2026 with perhaps one token cut in December. Equities deliver flat to low-single-digit returns. Commodities (energy, gold) outperform. Credit spreads widen further.

**Probability:** 40%

**Risk scenario (25%):** Rapid conflict de-escalation + OPEC+ surge → oil drops below $80 → inflation concerns ease → Fed resumes cutting → equities rally strongly.

**Worst case (20%):** Hormuz closure, wider regional war, oil spikes above $150 → global recession, credit crisis, equities drop 20%+.

### Narrative Summary

The soft-landing narrative that dominated late 2025 and early 2026 has been **disrupted by an external shock**. The Iran-US/Israel conflict has introduced a supply-side inflation shock at a time when the Fed was already moving cautiously on rate cuts. Markets are repricing for a world where energy costs are structurally higher, growth is slower, and policy has less flexibility. The critical question is duration: a 2-3 month conflict with contained supply disruption is manageable; a prolonged regional war with Strait of Hormuz disruption fundamentally changes the macro calculus. For now, the data says **caution** — reduce risk, maintain commodity exposure, and wait for clarity on the geopolitical front before adding to equity positions.
