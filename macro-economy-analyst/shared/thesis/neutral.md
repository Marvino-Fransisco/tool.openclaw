Extreme sentiment readings (13/100 Fear & Greed) have historically been medium-term bullish signals — the crowd could be right in direction but wrong in magnitude, and a relief rally could emerge from oversold conditions even without resolution.

3. **The data gap matters more than the neutral case assumes.** April-May CPI, PMI, and employment data do not yet exist. If the oil shock proves transitory (prices normalize as supply routes adjust), the stagflation thesis could collapse in hard data, triggering a rapid regime change. The neutral case is built on lagging indicators that may not confirm the narrative.

4. **Structural liquidity support may emerge.** If the Treasury reduces issuance, the Fed slows QT, or foreign central banks provide dollar liquidity (as in prior stress episodes), the contractionary liquidity thesis weakens. The neutral case assumes no policy intervention, but crisis-adjacent environments historically attract policy responses.
